# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Casual budgeters who want straightforward expense tracking without bank connections or subscriptions. They care about privacy and simplicity — no accounts, no servers, no onboarding wizard. Their primary question on every visit: "What can I spend right now?"

## Product Purpose

A lightweight, privacy-first budgeting PWA for tracking accounts, budgets, bills, and savings goals. Data stays on-device (localStorage) with optional GitHub Gist sync for cross-device access. No bank links, no AI, no subscriptions — just envelope budgeting with typographic care.

## Brand Personality

Utility, confident, quiet. An editorial tool that gets out of your way. Warm blacks and amber data, not corporate blues and gradient cards. Numbers earn their weight; decoration earns its absence.

## Anti-references

- **Not SaaS-cream fintech**: No Mint, YNAB, or corporate finance aesthetics. No rounded cards with gradients, no hero-metric templates, no onboarding wizards.
- **Not dashboard-overload**: No gauges, sparklines, data viz flourishes, or "insights" panels. The available amount is the only chart that matters.
- **Not Notion-style soft-productivity**: No dot-grid backgrounds, pastel accents, or playful illustration style. This is a tool, not a lifestyle brand.

## Design Principles

1. **Privacy as a feature** — No accounts, no servers, no tracking. Every design choice reinforces that data stays on-device.
2. **Confidence through clarity** — The primary job is answering "what can I spend?" Every screen must make that answer immediate and unambiguous.
3. **Restraint over decoration** — Financial tools trend toward busy dashboards. Stack stays quiet. Information density serves the task, not the aesthetic.
4. **Zero-config start** — The app should feel useful on first load. No signup, no wizard, no onboarding tour.
5. **Editorial craft for daily use** — A daily driver deserves typographic care. Numbers in a dedicated mono face, hierarchy through weight and scale, not chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA compliance. Adequate color contrast, keyboard-navigable forms, and semantic HTML. Dark theme as default with amber accents that meet contrast thresholds against dark backgrounds.
